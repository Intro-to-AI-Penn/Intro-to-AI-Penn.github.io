Group Exercises

These weekly exercises are designed to help you apply core AI concepts in small teams. Each activity builds intuition about how AI systems learn, generate, and reason—while encouraging reflection on their societal impact.

Exercise List

Week Title Description
1 What Makes a Machine “Intelligent”? In small groups, brainstorm what “intelligence” means for humans and machines. Compare your ideas with early AI definitions and discuss examples.
2 Exploring Data Analyze a small dataset using visualization tools (e.g., Google Sheets or ChatGPT’s table analysis). Identify trends and anomalies.
3 Machine Learning in Action Use a simple web-based ML demo to fit a curve or classify points. Reflect on what the model learned and what it didn’t.
4 Neural Networks, Simplified Work through a visual neural network simulation (like TensorFlow Playground). Adjust parameters and observe how performance changes.
5 Prompting with Generative AI Experiment with LLMs to create short creative or analytical outputs. Discuss how prompt structure influences the result.
8 Bias and Fairness in AI Review examples of biased model outputs. Discuss possible sources of bias in data, design, or deployment.
10 AI in the Real World Choose a field (healthcare, art, business, etc.) and outline one positive and one risky use of AI. Present your group’s findings.

Guidelines

  • Groups will typically consist of 3–5 students.
  • Participation in exercises counts toward your attendance and engagement grade.
  • Be prepared to share a short summary of your discussion or findings at the end of class.
  • When using AI tools, cite them clearly in your submissions.
